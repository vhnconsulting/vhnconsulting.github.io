Representative Greene Holds COVID-19 Vaccine Injury Congressional Hearing
 
Background: Congresswoman Marjorie Taylor Greene held a congressional hearing yesterday on “injuries caused by COVID-19 vaccines,” featuring several well-known vaccine opponents. Robert Malone testified that the vaccines were rushed and that COVID-19 was not as severe as the public believed. Anti-vaccine attorney Tom Renz, who previously falsely claimed that a nonexistent Medicare database held evidence of thousands of vaccine-related deaths, testified that a whistleblower claims to have received a Moderna COVID-19 vaccine in 2014. OG-GYN Kimberly Biss testified that her patients’ miscarriage rate doubled following the vaccine rollout. The hearing was live-streamed on multiple platforms.
 
Why this matters: This event is an example of vaccine opponents using congressional hearings as a platform to legitimize already debunked claims. Although clips and quotes from the hearing have not circulated much outside anti-vaccine circles, vaccine opponents will likely use the hearing to advance their narratives for months to come. 

Posts Baselessly Link Heart Damage to COVID-19 Vaccines

Background:  Several trending posts claim COVID-19 vaccines cause heart damage. A widely circulated video resurfaced the misleading claim that 1 in 35 Moderna vaccine recipients have heart damage. Vigilant Fox and Peter McCullough shared a high school’s flier for cardiac screenings for athletes, implying that a practice that has been in place for at least four decades is related to COVID-19 vaccines. Some vaccine opponents are attempting to link a rise in heart attacks in Australia to COVID-19 vaccines despite experts attributing the increase to COVID-19.

Why this matters:
False links between COVID-19 vaccines and heart issues have persisted for nearly as long as the vaccines have been available and have negatively impacted the public’s perception of the vaccines’ safety.


Misinformation abounds about fall vaccine safety

Background: Vaccine opponents are working overtime to advance the narrative that flu, COVID-19, and RSV vaccines are unsafe. Some posts argue against getting any combination of the vaccines, while others highlight those refusing the vaccines.

Why this matters: These posts highlight the continued opposition to fall vaccines that began this past summer.  While grouping seasonal vaccines may be beneficial for public health messaging, it may have the unintended effect of damaging the perception of existing routine vaccines among people who are skeptical about newer vaccines.
